🛡️ Complete Guide to QR Code Phishing (Quishing)

📌 What is Quishing?

Quishing (QR code phishing) is a cyberattack where malicious QR codes are used to trick users into visiting fake websites, downloading malware, or revealing sensitive information.

🔴 Real-World Attack Scenarios:

  • Parking Meter Scams: Fake QR codes placed on parking meters leading to fake payment pages
  • Restaurant Menu Attacks: QR codes on tables replaced with malicious versions
  • Email Attachments: QR codes in phishing emails bypassing spam filters
  • Package Delivery Scams: Fake delivery notices with QR codes
  • Job Offer Scams: QR codes in fake interview invitations

⚠️ How to Identify Suspicious QR Codes

✅ Inspection Checklist:

  1. Physical Inspection: Look for stickers over original codes
  2. URL Preview: Most scanners show the URL - check for misspellings
  3. HTTPS Check: Ensure the website uses encryption
  4. Domain Verification: Is the domain legitimate? (e.g., google.com vs g00gle.com)
  5. Unexpected Requests: Legitimate QR codes rarely ask for login credentials

🛡️ Protection Measures

🔒 Security Best Practices:

  • Use a QR scanner with built-in security features
  • Enable URL preview before automatically opening links
  • Install mobile security software with phishing protection
  • Never enter credentials after scanning an unexpected QR code
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all accounts
  • Report suspicious QR codes to security teams
  • Keep your device's OS and apps updated
